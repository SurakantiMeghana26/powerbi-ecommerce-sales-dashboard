## 🎯 Project Overview

This Power BI dashboard transforms Madhav Ecommerce's sales data into **actionable business intelligence** for Indian retail operations. The dashboard provides multi-dimensional analysis covering geographic distribution, payment preferences, product categories, and temporal patterns — enabling data-driven strategic decisions.

### 🌟 Project Highlights

- ✨ **₹438K Total Sales** analyzed across Indian states
- ✨ **Quarterly Filtering** for time-based analysis
- ✨ **Customer-Level Insights** for top buyers
- ✨ **Payment Method Analysis** for digital strategy
- ✨ **8 Integrated Visualizations** in a dark-themed design
- ✨ **Geographic Coverage** across multiple Indian states

### 🎯 Business Objective

Enable Madhav Ecommerce stakeholders to:
- 📊 Track overall sales and profitability metrics
- 🌍 Identify high-performing states and regions
- 👥 Understand top customer purchasing patterns
- 💳 Analyze payment method preferences
- 📦 Optimize product category strategy
- 📅 Identify seasonal trends and patterns

## 🔑 Key Metrics (KPIs)

The dashboard features **4 critical KPIs** providing executive-level insights:

| KPI | Value | What It Measures |
|-----|-------|------------------|
| 💰 **Sum of Amount** | ₹438K | Total revenue generated |
| 💵 **Sum of Profit** | ₹37K | Net profit earned |
| 📦 **Sum of Quantity** | 5,615 units | Total items sold |
| 💲 **Sum of AOV** | ₹121K | Average Order Value |

### 💡 KPI Analysis

- **Profit Margin:** ~8.5% (₹37K profit on ₹438K sales)
- **Volume Performance:** 5,615 units indicates good market traction
- **Customer Spending:** High AOV (₹121K) suggests premium customer base
- **Growth Opportunity:** Profit margin could be optimized

## 📈 Dashboard Components

### 1️⃣ Sum of Amount by State (Geographic Distribution)

📊 **Visualization:** Horizontal bar chart

**Top Performing States:**

| Rank | State | Sales |
|------|-------|-------|
| 1 | 🥇 **Maharashtra** | Highest (~₹50K+) |
| 2 | 🥈 **Madhya Pradesh** | Strong performance |
| 3 | 🥉 **Uttar Pradesh** | Significant share |
| 4 | **Delhi** | Capital region presence |
| 5 | **Gujarat** | Western market |

**Key Insight:** Maharashtra dominates sales, likely due to Mumbai/Pune metro presence. Tier-2 cities in MP and UP show strong potential.

### 2️⃣ Sum of Quantity by Category (Product Mix)

📊 **Visualization:** Donut chart

**Category Distribution:**

| Category | Share | Units |
|----------|-------|-------|
| 👕 **Clothing** | **62.62%** | Dominant category |
| 📺 **Electronics** | 20.55% | Second largest |
| 🪑 **Furniture** | 16.83% | Smallest segment |

**Key Insight:** Clothing is the clear winner with 62.62% market share, indicating strong fashion/apparel demand. This is consistent with Indian ecommerce trends.

### 3️⃣ Sum of Profit by Month (Monthly Trend)

📊 **Visualization:** Vertical column chart

**Monthly Patterns:**

- 📈 **Peak Months:** January, March, November
- 📉 **Low Months:** May, June, July (likely off-season)
- 🎯 **Festive Boost:** October-December shows strong performance
- ⚠️ **Loss Months:** July, December show negative profit

**Key Insight:** Seasonal patterns align with Indian shopping festivals (Diwali, wedding seasons). July's loss may indicate monsoon impact or aggressive discounting.

### 4️⃣ Sum of Amount by Customer Name

📊 **Visualization:** Bar chart of top customers

**Top Customers:**

| Customer | Performance |
|----------|-------------|
| 🏆 **Harivansh** | Top spender (~10K) |
| 🥈 **Madhav** | Second highest |
| 🥉 **Madan Mohan** | Strong contributor |
| **Shiva** | Notable customer |

**Key Insight:** A small group of high-value customers contributes significantly to revenue — perfect target for loyalty programs and personalized marketing.

### 5️⃣ Sum of Quantity by Payment Mode

📊 **Visualization:** Donut chart with breakdown

**Payment Method Distribution:**

| Payment Mode | Share | Strategic Implication |
|--------------|-------|----------------------|
| 💵 **COD (Cash on Delivery)** | **43.74%** | Trust factor critical |
| 📱 **UPI** | 20.61% | Digital payments growing |
| 💳 **Debit Card** | 13.20% | Banking integration |
| 💳 **Credit Card** | 11.97% | Premium customers |
| 📅 **EMI** | 10.49% | High-value purchases |

**Critical Insight:** **COD dominates at 43.74%** — typical of Indian ecommerce. Reducing COD share through digital incentives could improve cash flow significantly.

### 6️⃣ Sum of Profit by Sub-Category (Product Performance)

📊 **Visualization:** Horizontal bar chart

**Top Profit Generators:**

| Rank | Sub-Category | Profit Performance |
|------|--------------|-------------------|
| 1 | 🖨️ **Printers** | Highest (10K) |
| 2 | 📚 **Bookcases** | Strong second |
| 3 | 👘 **Sarees** | Cultural product strength |
| 4 | 🎒 **Accessories** | Steady contributor |
| 5 | 🪑 **Tables** | Furniture base |

**Key Insight:** Printers and Bookcases lead profit margins, while Sarees show India-specific cultural product strength.

### 7️⃣ Interactive Quarterly Filters

🎛️ **Dynamic Slicers:**

- **Qtr 1** (Jan-Mar)
- **Qtr 2** (Apr-Jun)
- **Qtr 3** (Jul-Sep)
- **Qtr 4** (Oct-Dec)
- **All** (Default view)

These quarter-based filters enable seasonal analysis and quarterly performance comparison.

## 💡 Key Insights

### 🎯 Top 6 Business Insights

#### 1. **Geographic Concentration** 🌍
- **Maharashtra leads** in total sales contribution
- **Northern states** (MP, UP, Delhi) form a strong cluster
- **Tier-2 cities** show significant purchasing power
- **Western India** dominates the customer base

#### 2. **Product Category Dominance** 👕
- **Clothing represents 62.62%** of quantity sold
- Indian consumers prefer apparel over electronics/furniture
- **Strong seasonal correlation** with festivals
- Fashion category drives volume but may have lower margins

#### 3. **Payment Behavior** 💳
- **COD dominates at 43.74%** — typical Indian ecommerce pattern
- **Digital payments combined** (UPI + Cards + EMI) = 56.27%
- **EMI usage at 10.49%** indicates significant high-value purchases
- **UPI growth** reflects digital India transformation

#### 4. **Customer Segmentation** 👥
- **Top 4 customers** (Harivansh, Madhav, Madan Mohan, Shiva) drive significant revenue
- **High AOV (₹121K)** indicates premium positioning
- Opportunity for **personalized marketing**
- **Loyalty program potential** for VIP customers

#### 5. **Seasonal Patterns** 📅
- **Festival seasons drive sales** (Oct-Dec)
- **Monsoon months underperform** (Jul-Aug)
- **Q1 strong performance** (post-New Year + Republic Day)
- **Mid-year slowdown** typical of Indian retail

#### 6. **Sub-Category Profit Leaders** 💰
- **Printers and Bookcases** lead profit generation
- **Sarees represent strong India-specific product line**
- **Cross-category mix** indicates diversified portfolio
- Furniture sub-categories perform consistently

## 🎯 Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis:

### 🚀 Growth Opportunities

1. **Reduce COD Dependency**
   - Offer 2-3% discount on digital payments
   - Promote UPI for instant transactions
   - Reduces logistics costs and cash handling

2. **Expand into Underserved States**
   - Karnataka, Tamil Nadu showing low presence
   - South Indian market untapped
   - Consider regional language support

3. **Optimize Monsoon Months**
   - July-August lowest performance
   - Launch monsoon-specific campaigns
   - Indoor product promotions

### 💡 Strategic Initiatives

1. **Customer Loyalty Program**
   - VIP tier for top customers (Harivansh, Madhav, etc.)
   - Personalized recommendations
   - Exclusive deals for repeat buyers

2. **Festive Season Strategy**
   - Maximize Oct-Dec opportunity
   - Wedding season campaigns
   - Special promotions for Diwali, Dussehra

3. **Category-Specific Marketing**
   - **Clothing:** Volume focus (62.62% share)
   - **Electronics:** Premium positioning
   - **Furniture:** Bundle deals

4. **EMI Promotion**
   - 10.49% already use EMI
   - Promote for high-ticket items
   - Partner with banks for offers

## 🚀 How to Use

### Prerequisites

- ✅ Microsoft Power BI Desktop (free download)
- ✅ Windows 10/11

### Steps

**1️⃣ Clone the repository**

```bash
git clone https://github.com/SurakantiMeghana26/powerbi-ecommerce-sales-dashboard.git
cd powerbi-ecommerce-sales-dashboard
```

**2️⃣ Download Power BI Desktop** (if not installed)

Visit: [Power BI Desktop Download](https://powerbi.microsoft.com/en-us/desktop/)

**3️⃣ Open the .pbix file**

Double-click `Sales Analytics Dashboard.pbix` to open in Power BI Desktop.

**4️⃣ Interact with the dashboard**

- Click **Qtr 1, Qtr 2, Qtr 3, or Qtr 4** to filter by quarter
- Click **"All"** to see complete data
- Click on **chart segments** for cross-filtering
- Hover over visuals for **detailed tooltips**

**5️⃣ Explore underlying data**

- Switch to **Data view** to see tables
- Check **Model view** for relationships
- Review **DAX measures** in calculated fields

## 🎯 Dataset Information

### Source
**Madhav Ecommerce Sales Dataset** — Indian retail data covering:

- 🌍 **Geography:** Multiple Indian states (Maharashtra, MP, UP, Delhi, Gujarat, etc.)
- 📦 **Categories:** Clothing, Electronics, Furniture
- 💳 **Payment Modes:** COD, UPI, Credit/Debit Card, EMI
- 👥 **Customers:** Multiple buyers across India
- 📅 **Time Period:** Quarterly data (Q1-Q4)

### Data Fields Include
- Order details (Order ID, Customer Name, Date)
- Customer information (Name, State, City)
- Product details (Category, Sub-Category)
- Financial metrics (Amount, Profit, Quantity)
- Payment information (Payment Mode)
- Temporal data (Order Date, Quarter, Month)
